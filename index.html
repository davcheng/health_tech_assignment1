2. Balloon betting Analogue Risk Test (BART)

Mean Pumps Comparison (after explosion/after no explosion)

In the Balloon betting analogue risk test, the first point visualized depicts the comparison between number of pumps following an explosion versus the number following no explosion (collection). The results show that in almost all circumstances, the subject was more risk adverse following an explosion, with a resulting pump average of 2.87 post-explosion versus 3.77 post-no-explosion.

Number of Explosions vs. Range of Pumps

The above graph illustrates a nuanced example of user callibration. In the beginning, the user is unsure of how many explosions is considered "good" or "bad". After 7 explosions, the user lowers his risk-profile (highlighted by the low number of pumps), and after seeing a decreased number of explosions, increases his risk tolerance. Eventually, the user is able to increase the range of pumps over the number of explosions (reversing the initial trend).

Standard Deviation vs. Mean Pumps

The graph of standard deviation and mean pumps illustrates the user's playing style. The first trial ran had very little standard deviation, due to initial apprehension caused by unfamiliarity with the game. The clustering of 6 trials around 3.3 pumps illustrates that, regardless of standard deviation, the user will tend to stick to a "strategy", regardless of intentionality.
